The Income-tax department has issued notices to over 50 car dealers, asking them to furnish details of customers who have bought cars after November 8, following the demonetisation of 500- and 1,000-rupee currency notes. The notices are being sent under Section 133(6) of the Income Tax Act, 1961, which empowers the I-T authorities to seek information that could be useful in connection with any pending proceeding or enquiry. Confirming the development, a senior income-tax official said that the department has observed a significant increase in car sales during the past two months. "This is part of an exercise in which we checking the use of junk old notes and also tax evasion through such buying," the official said. The I-T notices call upon car dealers to provide details of customers along with their addresses, make and model numbers of the four-wheelers they've bought, invoiced cost, booking dates, among other details. ADVERTISING The department also has asked car dealers to provide ledgers of all customers who have booked vehicles from November 1 till date, along with bank account details with statements during that period. The notices advise car dealers not to tender demonetised currencies for any such transactions and inform the tax authorities in case customers ask for such deals through old notes. In the backdrop of the demonetisation drive, the department suspects that the old currency has been used to buy cars, an income-tax source said. POLICIES:

  • At the wake of the Independence Day 2021, honorable PM Modi has launched the significant vehicle scrappage policy and urged the startups and youths to join this mission to curb emission of the pollution as well as reduce the blow of Covid-19 pandemic on the Indian automobile sector. If you have a vehicle nearing end of life, then this article is of special importance to you. With your vehicle running for years now, you might plan to renew it or get the parts replaced to re-hit the road. Have you thought about scraping it? Well, it might be time for you to bid adieu to your aged vehicle and get a new one at amazing discounts. Let’s find out what this policy is about in brief.
  • Know about the Vehicle Scrappage Policy 2021 Vehicle scrapping policy will help phase out pollution-emitting and unfit vehicles in an environment-friendly manner. As per law, a registration certificate for a passenger vehicle is valid for 15 years and 10 years for a commercial vehicle.
  • So, what all you need to know about vehicle scrapping? The scrappage policy will mandate vehicles to undergo fitness test after the expiry of the registration period every year. Fitness exams are required to understand the quality of the vehicle if it is still fit to run on the road and how much effect it will have on the environment. Vehicles will not be just scrapped based on the age, rather they will be put through scientifically tried automated testing stops.
  • What if your vehicle doesn’t pass fitness test? The term unfit vehicles refer to those who fail to pass a fitness test; have been damaged in the fire, riot, natural disaster, declared obsolete or beyond economic repair. A vehicle which does not pass the fitness test is considered unfit from plying on the roads and their registration certificate is no more renewed. However, a single re-examination is allowed after the required repair, rectification and re-inspection- if at all allowed by the appellate authority.
